# Elsevier Journal Recommendations

This note gives a practical submission order for the current manuscript package. The ranking is based on topical fit first, then on editorial realism for the paper in its current form.

Update note:

- The manuscript has since been rebuilt around a stronger IMRAD structure, validation-selected main tables, explicit limitations, and a real literature-grounded related-work section.
- That rewrite strengthens the fit for `Neurocomputing` and makes `Neural Networks` a more credible second option than it was under the earlier skeleton draft.

Verification date: May 1, 2026

Important notes:

- This is a strategy recommendation, not a guarantee of acceptance.
- The ranking is based on official journal scope pages and current journal insights.
- The manuscript is positioned as a controlled architectural benchmark, not as a state-of-the-art vision paper.

## 1. Neurocomputing

Why it is the best first target:

- The journal explicitly covers neural architectures, learning methods, biological neural network modeling, machine learning, and pattern recognition.
- The paper's strongest identity is a controlled benchmark on a biologically inspired neural architecture family.
- The current manuscript tone already matches this journal well.

Current official indicators:

- CiteScore: 13.6
- Impact Factor: 6.5
- Submission to first decision: 8 days
- Submission to acceptance: 139 days

Fit summary:

- Best balance of topical fit, editorial realism, and audience alignment.

Official pages:

- Journal page: https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/neurocomputing
- Elsevier LaTeX guidance: https://www.elsevier.com/en-gb/researcher/author/policies-and-guidelines/latex-instructions

## 2. Neural Networks

Why it is very strong but more selective:

- The journal explicitly welcomes work spanning biologically inspired artificial intelligence, computational neuroscience, and engineering applications where neural networks are central.
- Your manuscript's dendritic computation framing fits naturally here.
- However, the journal may expect a stronger literature integration and a sharper theoretical positioning than the current draft yet provides.

Current official indicators:

- CiteScore: 10.6
- Impact Factor: 6.3
- Submission to first decision: 9 days
- Submission to acceptance: 208 days

Fit summary:

- Excellent conceptual fit, but likely a harder editorial path than Neurocomputing.

Official page:

- https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/neural-networks

## 3. Applied Soft Computing

Why it is a strong fallback:

- The journal covers neural computing, machine and deep learning, and computational methods aimed at robustness and low solution cost.
- The manuscript's parameter-efficiency and controlled comparison angle fits this scope well.
- It is especially suitable if the paper is framed as efficient neural classification under architectural inductive bias rather than as a neuroscience-oriented contribution.

Current official indicators:

- CiteScore: 14.5
- Impact Factor: 6.6
- APC for optional open access: USD 3,560

Fit summary:

- Broadly suitable and practical, especially if the final narrative emphasizes efficient learning rather than biological interpretation.

Official page:

- https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/applied-soft-computing

## 4. Knowledge-Based Systems

Why it remains a credible alternative:

- The journal emphasizes innovative artificial intelligence techniques, balanced theory-practice coverage, and intelligent models and methods.
- The paper can fit if framed as reproducible neural architecture evaluation and parameter-efficient intelligent model design.
- A strong public code and data package would improve fit here.

Current official indicators:

- CiteScore: 15.0
- Impact Factor: 7.6
- Submission to first decision: 3 days
- Submission to acceptance: 158 days

Fit summary:

- Good alternative if the paper is written more as an AI-methods evaluation paper than as a biologically inspired neural-computation paper.

Official page:

- https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/knowledge-based-systems

## 5. Information Sciences

Why it is fifth:

- The journal covers intelligent systems, artificial neural networks, learning and evolutionary computing, and architecture evaluation more broadly.
- It is still a real fit, but it is less naturally centered on dendritic neural computation than the four journals above.
- The paper would need especially polished related work and positioning to feel fully at home there.

Current official indicators:

- CiteScore: 14.4
- Impact Factor: 6.8
- Submission to first decision: 2 days
- Submission to acceptance: 170 days

Fit summary:

- Viable, but not the most natural first home for the present manuscript.

Official page:

- https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/information-sciences

## Recommended submission order

1. Neurocomputing
2. Neural Networks
3. Applied Soft Computing
4. Knowledge-Based Systems
5. Information Sciences

## Strategic writing note

The manuscript is strongest when presented as:

- controlled architectural evidence
- parameter-efficient neural classification
- biologically inspired but not overclaimed
- honest about mixed and negative results
- explicit about timing and baseline discipline

The manuscript is weaker if presented as:

- a universal replacement for standard neural networks
- a state-of-the-art image classifier
- a definitive low-data robustness paper based on the current historical low-data folders
